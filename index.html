The Science Behind Chocolate

Why We Love It

Melts in the Mouth

Chocolate melts close to body temperature (around 93°F), which is why it feels incredibly smooth, luxurious, and creamy when eaten.

The Roasting Magic

Roasting cocoa beans develops their final flavor. Heat creates complex chemical reactions that unlock bold, deep, and earthy notes.

Cocoa Percentage

A higher cocoa percentage means more natural cocoa mass and fewer sweet additives, resulting in a rich, bitter, and intense flavor profile.

The History of Chocolate

A Timeless Discovery

1500 BCE

Cocoa is used in ancient cultures and early civilizations, consumed largely as a bitter, sacred drink.

1600s

Chocolate arrives and is introduced to Europe, spreading among the nobility.

1800s

Key technological advancements lead to the invention of the modern pressed chocolate bar.

1900s

The modern chocolate industry grows, making sweet premium chocolate universally accessible.
